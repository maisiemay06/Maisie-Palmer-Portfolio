As a self-taught front-end developer with 19 months of hands-on experience at a dynamic tech startup, I have not only honed my coding skills but also developed a deep passion for creating seamless and user-friendly web applications. My journey into the world of coding has been driven by self-motivation and a relentless desire to learn and innovate.
My expertise extends to Vue.js, Nuxt.js, and TailwindCSS, which I have mastered independently through dedication and continuous self-improvement. I believe that the ability to self-learn and adapt in the fast-paced tech industry is a valuable asset, and I am committed to keeping up with the ever-evolving web development landscape.
Currently, I am 18 months into a comprehensive 2-year Level 4 Software Developer Apprenticeship with The Coders Guild (more information available below), a program that has not only reinforced my skills but also provided me with a strong foundation in software development principles beyond just those I encounter daily in my current role. I am excited to continue working towards completing this apprenticeship to further enhance my knowledge and capabilities in the field.
In my next professional endeavor, I am eager to find a role that allows me to build upon my apprenticeship and continue my growth as a front-end developer. My ideal job would involve working with Vue.js, as it aligns perfectly with my current skill set and passion. I am dedicated to contributing to a dynamic team, driving innovation, and ensuring exceptional user experiences through cutting-edge web applications. I am excited to join a forward-thinking company where I can continue to learn and grow, while making a valuable contribution to the company.

Apprenticeship

I'm currently in the midst of pursuing a Level 4 Apprenticeship program with The Coders Guild. I've already dedicated 19 months to this and have about 5 months left to complete the apprenticeship. As part of my commitment, I must allocate at least 6 wokring hours per week to learning and developing new skills while working on building a comprehensive written portfolio. One this is completed (estimated completion mid-December) , the apprenticeship requires me to undertake an End Point Assessment (EPA), which is anticipated to take around 7 weeks to complete. This EPA involves working independently on a work based project set by my manager, to be carried out during regular work hours.
